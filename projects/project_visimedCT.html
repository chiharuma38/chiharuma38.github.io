VisiMed CT — Medical Imaging Innovation

Medical imaging solution developed and pitched at the 2024 Capital Region Bioinnovation Bootcamp. Finalist — a workflow for safer, more accessible CT diagnostics.

Medical Imaging Innovation Entrepreneurship Bootcamp Finalist Biomedical Engineering

Project Overview

Developed a concept and pitch for VisiMed CT — a workflow improvement system designed to make CT diagnostics safer and more accessible, particularly in under-resourced clinical settings. Presented as a finalist at the 2024 Capital Region Bioinnovation Bootcamp.

  • Problem: CT radiation exposure is a significant concern, particularly for pediatric and repeat-scan patients. Access to radiologists for real-time interpretation is also limited in many settings.
  • Solution: A structured workflow combining automated pre-scan checklists, dose optimization protocols, and AI-assisted preliminary read flagging to reduce errors and radiation exposure.
  • Outcome: Finalist at the Capital Region Bioinnovation Bootcamp 2024 — competed against teams from across the region.

Technical and Business Highlights

  • Market research and clinical literature review to validate the problem and size the opportunity.
  • Stakeholder interviews with radiologists and hospital administrators to refine the workflow design.
  • Financial modeling and go-to-market strategy developed as part of the bootcamp curriculum.
  • Pitch deck and live presentation to a panel of judges from clinical, investment, and industry backgrounds.

Lessons Learned

  • Clinical problems require deep stakeholder empathy — the technical solution is rarely the hardest part.
  • Regulatory pathways (FDA 510k, CE marking) significantly shape what's feasible for medical device startups.
  • This project sparked my interest in the entrepreneurial and investment side of engineering — the gap between a good idea and a funded company is almost entirely about people and communication.
