Judo Flip Trajectory Analysis

Mathematical modeling and centroid tracking to analyze flip trajectories in Judo — comparing expert and novice movement patterns using Python and MATLAB.

MATLAB Python Biomechanics Computer Vision Data Analysis

Project Overview

Analyzed the biomechanics of Judo throwing techniques by tracking body centroid trajectories from video footage of expert and novice practitioners. Mathematical modeling identified key differences in movement patterns that correlate with technique quality.

  • Video Processing: Frame-by-frame centroid tracking of thrower and receiver using Python OpenCV.
  • Trajectory Modeling: Fitted polynomial curves to centroid paths to extract quantitative trajectory features.
  • Comparative Analysis: Statistical comparison of trajectory parameters between expert and novice groups.
  • MATLAB Visualization: Plotted trajectory overlays and statistical distributions for presentation.

Key Findings

  • Expert practitioners showed tighter, more consistent centroid trajectories across repeated throws.
  • The peak height of the receiver's centroid during the throw correlates with technique efficiency.
  • Hip rotation timing was the most discriminating feature between skill levels.
  • Results suggest centroid tracking could be used as an objective coaching feedback tool.

Lessons Learned

  • Video quality and consistent camera angles are critical for reliable centroid tracking.
  • Biomechanical analysis bridges engineering and sports science in useful ways.
  • Mathematical modeling can make subjective coaching observations quantitative and reproducible.
