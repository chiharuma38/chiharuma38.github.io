Arthroscope-Camera CAD Design

Surgical arthroscope and integrated camera system designed in SolidWorks for orthopedic applications, focusing on ergonomics, manufacturability, and sterilization compatibility.

SolidWorks CAD Medical Devices Orthopedics Biomedical Engineering

Project Overview

Designed a complete arthroscope and camera system assembly in SolidWorks, intended for minimally invasive orthopedic surgery. The design prioritizes surgeon ergonomics, instrument channel compatibility, and autoclave sterilization requirements.

  • Scope Design: 4mm diameter rigid arthroscope with 30-degree field of view — standard for knee and shoulder procedures.
  • Camera Integration: Integrated camera head with coupler designed to attach to standard 1/3-inch CCD camera modules.
  • Ergonomics: Handle geometry optimized for single-handed grip during sustained surgical procedures.
  • Manufacturability: All components designed with standard machining tolerances and biocompatible material specifications (316L stainless steel, medical-grade polycarbonate).

Technical Highlights

  • Full parametric SolidWorks assembly with mating constraints and interference checking.
  • Technical drawings produced with GD&T annotations for manufacturing.
  • FEA stress analysis on the scope shaft under lateral load conditions — verified structural integrity within safe limits.
  • Bill of materials generated with material specifications and estimated unit cost.

Lessons Learned

  • Medical device design is heavily constrained by sterilization requirements — material choices are limited.
  • Ergonomic design requires iterative prototyping and feedback — CAD alone is insufficient.
  • Tolerance stack-up analysis is critical for assemblies with multiple mating components.
