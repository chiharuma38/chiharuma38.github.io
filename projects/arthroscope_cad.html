Arthroscope-Camera CAD Design

Project Summary

This project involved the design and modeling of a medical arthroscope with an integrated camera system using SolidWorks. The goal was to create realistic, manufacturable, and ergonomically designed parts that could be assembled virtually, simulating a real medical device used in minimally invasive orthopedic procedures.

  • Complex Geometry: Designed intricate camera and arthroscope parts using lofts, extrudes, advanced sketching, and creative problem-solving.
  • Iterative Problem-Solving: Overcame challenges in cutting curved surfaces, creating spikes and fillets, and ensuring correct dimensions and fit in assembly.
  • Full Assembly: Modeled and assembled all major arthroscope parts and connectors, testing for fit, function, and realism.
  • Manufacturability: Paid close attention to dimensioning, interfaces, and constraints for real-world prototyping and usability.

Technical Highlights

  • SolidWorks Skills: Used advanced features (lofted boss/base, multi-plane sketching, split lines, 3D sketch, and cut-extrudes) for complex shapes.
  • Modular Part Design: Created each part as a separate file and combined them in a master assembly for flexibility and clarity.
  • Challenges Overcome: Developed creative solutions for cutting even surfaces on curved models, properly dimensioning assemblies, and managing multiple file relationships.
  • Project Documentation: Maintained a detailed log of modeling steps, problems, and solutions for each component.

Lessons Learned

  • Careful planning and stepwise modeling are vital for complex assemblies—start with basic shapes, add detail iteratively.
  • Documenting modeling steps and challenges is essential for troubleshooting and future reference.
  • SolidWorks’ advanced features are powerful for medical device design but require precision and patience.
  • Practical manufacturability and assembly constraints are as important as visual aesthetics.
  • Testing and learning from failed attempts (especially with curved/filleted cuts) leads to improved modeling skills.
