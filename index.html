Background · About me

I'm a power systems engineer working at the intersection of grid digitalization, renewable energy integration, and Python-based automation.

At Siemens I automated PSS®E and TSAT workflows in Python, cutting contingency analysis runtime by 70% and simulation runtime by 94% through parallel processing. At GE Vernova I performed short-circuit, load flow, and transient stability studies in PSS®E and PowerFactory for utility and industrial clients across the US.

In August 2026 I start the SENSE MSc at KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Year 1) followed by KU Leuven in Belgium (Year 2), as part of the InnoEnergy dual degree programme. Focus: offshore wind grid integration, HVDC, and smart grid control.

Longer term I'm drawn to the intersection of power engineering and capital allocation · the technical due diligence and infrastructure investment building the energy transition.
